Moving to Bigger Numbers!

You know about Ones, Tens, Hundreds, and Thousands. Let's explore even bigger numbers!

Hello, 5-Digit Numbers!

What happens if we have the biggest 4-digit number, 9,999, and add just one more?

We get 10,000! This is the smallest 5-digit number, called Ten Thousand.

Think of it like having 10 bags, and each bag has 1,000 marbles. That's 10,000 marbles!

Welcome, 6-Digit Numbers!

Now, what if we have the biggest 5-digit number, 99,999, and add one more?

We get 1,00,000! This is the smallest 6-digit number, which we already learned is called One Lakh.

Remember the families (periods)?

  • Ones Period: Hundreds, Tens, Ones (H T O)
  • Thousands Period: Ten Thousands, Thousands (TTh Th)
  • Lakhs Period: Lakhs (L)

Commas are Your Friends!

Commas help us read these big numbers. We put a comma after the Lakhs place and after the Thousands place.

Example: 3,54,729

  • The first comma separates Lakhs (3) from Thousands (54).
  • The second comma separates Thousands (54) from Ones (729).

This helps us read it as: "Three lakh, fifty-four thousand, seven hundred twenty-nine."

Writing Numbers: Words to Digits

Can you write the number when you hear the words?

  • "Thirty-six thousand, four hundred four" becomes 36,404. (Notice the zero needed in the tens place!)
  • "Six lakh, six" becomes 6,00,006. (Lots of zeros needed here!)
  • "Nine lakh, fifty-seven thousand" becomes 9,57,000. (Zeros needed for the Ones period!)

Writing Numbers: Digits to Words

You can also write the number names from the digits.

Example: 5,82,314 is "Five lakh, eighty-two thousand, three hundred fourteen".

Stretching Big Numbers (Expanded Form)

We can stretch out big numbers too!

Example: Let's stretch 82,743.

  • 8 is in the Ten Thousands place = 80,000
  • 2 is in the Thousands place = 2,000
  • 7 is in the Hundreds place = 700
  • 4 is in the Tens place = 40
  • 3 is in the Ones place = 3

So, it's: 80,000 + 2,000 + 700 + 40 + 3

Big Numbers in Real Life

You see 5 and 6-digit numbers often! Think about:

  • The length of long rivers (maybe thousands of meters).
  • The amount on an electricity bill (maybe thousands of rupees).
  • The cost of cars (often in lakhs of rupees).
