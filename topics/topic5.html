Who Wins? Comparing Numbers!

Imagine playing a game with number cards. How do you know who has the bigger number? Let's learn the rules!

We use special signs to compare:

  • > means "Greater Than" (The crocodile mouth eats the bigger number!)
  • < means "Less Than"
  • = means "Equal To"

Rule 1: Count the Digits!

This is the easiest rule! The number with more digits is always bigger.

Example: Compare 75,431 (5 digits) and 1,20,798 (6 digits).

Since 1,20,798 has more digits, it's the bigger number!

So, 75,431 < 1,20,798

Rule 2: Same Number of Digits? Look Left!

If two numbers have the same number of digits, start comparing from the leftmost digit (the highest place value).

  1. Look at the first digit on the left in both numbers.
  2. The number with the bigger digit here is the bigger number.
  3. If the first digits are the same, move to the next digit to the right and compare those.
  4. Keep moving right until you find digits that are different. The bigger digit wins!

Example 1: Compare 7,30,415 and 2,79,081

  • Both have 6 digits.
  • Look at the leftmost digit (Lakhs place): 7 and 2.
  • Since 7 is bigger than 2, the first number is bigger.

So, 7,30,415 > 2,79,081

Example 2: Compare 4,12,892 and 4,83,005

  • Both have 6 digits.
  • Look at the leftmost digit (Lakhs place): 4 and 4. They are the same!
  • Move to the next digit (Ten Thousands place): 1 and 8.
  • Since 8 is bigger than 1, the second number is bigger.

So, 4,12,892 < 4,83,005

Comparing Helps with Ordering!

Once you know how to compare numbers, putting them in order is easy!

  • Ascending Order: Arranging numbers from smallest to greatest (like climbing up stairs).
  • Descending Order: Arranging numbers from greatest to smallest (like sliding down).

You just compare the numbers pair by pair to find the smallest or biggest one first!
