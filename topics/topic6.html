Lining Up Numbers!

Now that you're a champ at comparing numbers (knowing which is bigger or smaller), putting them in order is super easy!

Imagine lining up your friends from shortest to tallest, or tallest to shortest. We can do the same with numbers!

Ascending Order: Going Up!

Ascending order means arranging numbers from the smallest to the greatest.

Think of climbing up stairs: 1, 2, 3, 4...

Example: Let's arrange 500, 150, 800 in ascending order.

  1. Compare the numbers: 150 is the smallest.
  2. Next smallest is 500.
  3. The greatest is 800.

So, the ascending order is: 150, 500, 800

Descending Order: Going Down!

Descending order means arranging numbers from the greatest to the smallest.

Think of sliding down a slide: 10, 9, 8, 7...

Example: Let's arrange 500, 150, 800 in descending order.

  1. Compare the numbers: 800 is the greatest.
  2. Next greatest is 500.
  3. The smallest is 150.

So, the descending order is: 800, 500, 150

Ordering Big Numbers

It works the same way for big numbers! Just use your comparison skills.

Example: Arrange the river lengths 4,500m, 6,200m, 3,900m in descending order (longest to shortest).

  • Compare: 6,200 is the biggest.
  • Next biggest is 4,500.
  • Smallest is 3,900.

Descending order: 6,200m, 4,500m, 3,900m
