How Places Are Connected

Have you noticed how the place value houses (Ones, Tens, Hundreds...) are related? It's like they have a secret power-up rule!

The Magic of Ten!

Every time you move one step to the left on the place value chart, the value gets 10 times bigger!

  • You need 10 Ones to make 1 Ten. (10 x 1 = 10)
  • You need 10 Tens to make 1 Hundred. (10 x 10 = 100)
  • You need 10 Hundreds to make 1 Thousand. (10 x 100 = 1,000)
  • You need 10 Thousands to make 1 Ten Thousand. (10 x 1,000 = 10,000)
  • You need 10 Ten Thousands to make 1 Lakh. (10 x 10,000 = 1,00,000)

It's like climbing a ladder where each step is 10 times higher!

Ones → (x10) → Tens → (x10) → Hundreds → (x10) → Thousands → (x10) → Ten Thousands → (x10) → Lakhs

Think About It!

Knowing this "times 10" rule helps answer tricky questions:

  • How many Tens make one Thousand?
    • From Tens to Hundreds is x10.
    • From Hundreds to Thousands is x10.
    • So, from Tens to Thousands is x10 x10 = x100.
    • Answer: 100 tens make one thousand (100 x 10 = 1,000).
  • How many Hundreds make one Lakh?
    • Hundreds → (x10) → Thousands
    • Thousands → (x10) → Ten Thousands
    • Ten Thousands → (x10) → Lakhs
    • Total steps: x10 x10 x10 = x1000.
    • Answer: 1,000 hundreds make one lakh (1,000 x 100 = 1,00,000).
  • How many Thousands are there in 8 Lakhs?
    • From Thousands to Ten Thousands is x10.
    • From Ten Thousands to Lakhs is x10.
    • So, 1 Lakh has 10 x 10 = 100 Thousands.
    • Therefore, 8 Lakhs has 8 x 100 = 800 Thousands.
