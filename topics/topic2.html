Topic 2: Understanding Place Value (up to Lakhs)

What is Place Value?

Imagine numbers live in houses on a street. Place value tells us which house (or place) a digit lives in, and that tells us how much it's worth!

A digit like '7' can mean 7 ones, 7 tens, or even 7 hundreds, depending on its place!

Meet the Places!

Numbers have different places, just like houses on a street. Here are some important ones:

  • Ones (Like single candies)
  • Tens (Like packs of 10 candies)
  • Hundreds (Like boxes of 100 candies)
  • Thousands
  • Ten Thousands
  • Lakhs (This is a big place, like 100,000 candies!)

When we talk about the length of a river, we might use Thousands. When we talk about the price of something expensive, like a bike, we might use Lakhs.

What's a 'Lakh'?

A 'Lakh' is just a name for a big number: 100,000 (one hundred thousand).

It's like having 10 big bags, and each bag has 10,000 marbles inside. All together, that's 1 Lakh marbles!

Reading Big Numbers Easily

Big numbers look scary, but commas help! We group the places into families (called periods): Ones, Thousands, and Lakhs.

Commas (,) act like little fences between the families.

  • The Ones family: Hundreds, Tens, Ones (like H T O)
  • The Thousands family: Ten Thousands, Thousands (like TTh Th)
  • The Lakhs family: Lakhs (L)

Example: 6,45,321. The commas show us: 6 (Lakhs family), 45 (Thousands family), 321 (Ones family). So we read it: "Six lakh, forty-five thousand, three hundred twenty-one". Easy!

The Place Value Chart

A place value chart is like a special organizer for numbers. It has columns for each place (Ones, Tens, Hundreds...). When you put a number's digits in the chart, you can clearly see what each digit is worth.

Stretching Numbers Out (Expanded Form)

Expanded form is like stretching a number out to see the value of each digit.

Example: Let's stretch out 345.

  • The '3' is in the Hundreds place, so it's worth 300.
  • The '4' is in the Tens place, so it's worth 40.
  • The '5' is in the Ones place, so it's worth 5.

Stretched out, it's: 300 + 40 + 5. That equals 345!

Zero the Hero! (Placeholder)

Zero (0) might look like nothing, but it's a super important hero! It holds a place open when there's nothing to count for that place.

Example: How do we write "Six lakh, six"? We need the first '6' way over in the Lakhs place, and the other '6' in the Ones place.

We write it: 6,00,006.

The zeros are heroes holding the Ten Thousands, Thousands, Hundreds, and Tens places empty, so the 6s stay in the right spots!

Place Value Power-Ups!

Places are connected by the number 10!

  • 10 Ones power up to become 1 Ten!
  • 10 Tens power up to become 1 Hundred!
  • 10 Hundreds power up to become 1 Thousand!
  • ...and so on, up to 10 Ten Thousands powering up to 1 Lakh!

Knowing this helps when you add and subtract big numbers.
